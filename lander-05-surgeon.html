The Real Reason Skin Goes Loose — And Stays That Way

For years, I told patients: “Your skin stretched beyond its ability to snap back.” Standard explanation. Accepted. Repeated in every consulting room in the country.

But it’s incomplete.

The real mechanism is what researchers now call Elastin Fibre Collapse — a two-part failure that surgery simply cuts around rather than corrects:

1. Elastin fibres degrade and break. These are the proteins that give skin its snap. They break down through pregnancy, weight change, UV exposure, and age — and they cannot be rebuilt through diet or exercise alone.

2. Collagen production drops. After 35, most women produce 1–2% less collagen per year. Less collagen means thinner, less supported skin that gravity pulls down steadily over time.

Surgery cuts away the loose skin but does nothing to rebuild elastin or stimulate new collagen. Which is exactly why the laxity returns.
